# The ms2.fun Charter

---

## I. Purpose

ms2.fun is a curated protocol launchpad and the native deployment infrastructure for [noema.art](https://noema.art), a generative AI platform for NFT creation. The protocol treats AI as a first-class creative actor — both as a generative tool in the hands of human artists and as an autonomous agent capable of conceiving, deploying, and operating collections independently. Bespoke AI agents are recognized participants in the protocol on equal footing with human creators.

The protocol is founded on a single design principle: **on-chain alignment is a primitive**. Fees generated by derivative art projects are structurally routed to the communities those projects draw from. This relationship is not asserted by policy — it is enforced by code. Every deployed project routes fees into alignment vaults that buy and LP the aligned community's token.

The DAO governs the protocol's expansion surfaces. It approves alignment targets, factory types, and vault strategies. It does not direct artists, dictate creative output, or govern deployed instances.

---

## II. Governance Authority

GrandCentral, the protocol's Mol***-pattern DAO, holds exclusive authority over the following.

**Alignment targets.** The DAO approves and deactivates communities eligible to receive vault infrastructure. Approval requires a finding of genuine alignment: the proposing party must not hold controlling interest in the target token, and the community must maintain an independent constituency. Self-dealing disguised as alignment is grounds for rejection or deactivation.

**Factory types and vault strategies.** The DAO approves project factory templates and vault strategy contracts. Proposals require deployed, reviewable code. Approval is conditional on correctness and security evaluation. The DAO votes on artifacts, not applicants.

**Frontend infrastructure.** The DAO controls the protocol's decentralized frontend through the FrontendRegistry, an on-chain ENS controller that publishes versioned releases to IPFS. All frontend updates execute as DAO proposals through the 48-hour Timelock and are publicly visible on-chain before taking effect. No party may redirect the frontend without a passing governance proposal.

**Application process.** Proposals for new factory types, vault strategies, and alignment targets are permissionless. Any party may apply. Factory and vault proposals require deployed contracts. The DAO approves or rejects — it does not restrict who may propose.

---

## III. Protocol Commitments

**To aligned communities.**

The protocol makes two commitments to communities whose tokens receive vault infrastructure.

First, accurate representation. The protocol will not represent the alignment relationship as partnership, endorsement, or affiliation. The relationship is structural: vaults accumulate and LP the community's token as a function of project fees. Nothing more is implied; nothing less is delivered.

Second, ongoing stewardship. The DAO commits to monitoring approved alignment targets. A target that becomes harmful to artists, loses its independent constituency, or was approved under false pretenses is subject to deactivation. Approval is not permanent — it is maintained by ongoing DAO judgment.

**To artists, creators, and agents.**

The protocol makes four commitments to those who deploy instances — whether human artists, creative studios, or autonomous AI agents operating on behalf of a defined creative directive.

*Non-custodial infrastructure.* Deployers own their instances and their creative output. The protocol provides vaults, factories, and distribution infrastructure. It does not acquire ownership over any work — human or agent-authored.

*Immutable instance economics.* The economic parameters of a deployed instance — bonding fees, tithe rates, graduation splits — are fixed at deployment and cannot be altered by the DAO or any other party. Deployed instances operate under the terms agreed at creation for their full lifetime.

*Curated status.* Acceptance onto ms2.fun carries a verifiable signal of protocol-level review. The DAO's approval standards are the basis for that representation.

*Structural distribution.* Projects deployed on the protocol gain access to aligned communities as a distribution vector. This structural relationship is the protocol's primary value proposition to creators.

---

## IV. Protocol Evolution

The Charter does not fix the protocol's scope — it authorizes its expansion.

The DAO is empowered to add new alignment targets, factory types, and vault strategies, and to deactivate or deprecate any of these when circumstances warrant. The power to deactivate carries equal standing to the power to approve.

**Immutability of deployed instances.** One constraint is absolute: deployed instances are not subject to retroactive modification. The economic terms of live instances are honored in perpetuity. Governance applies forward — to new infrastructure — and does not revise existing deployments.

**Governance mechanics.** GrandCentral operates as a Mol***-pattern DAO. Shares carry voting power and are earned through contribution. Loot carries economic rights without governance weight. Members may ragequit at any time, burning shares and loot for a proportional claim on the treasury. Governance cannot trap capital.

All owner-gated protocol actions — upgrades, registrations, parameter changes — execute through a 48-hour Timelock. No protocol change is invisible. The community retains a standing window to observe, object, and exit before any change takes effect.
